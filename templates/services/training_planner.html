Training Planner

Design structured, effective training sessions that improve player skills, fitness, and tactical understanding throughout the season.

What is a Training Planner?

A training planner helps coaches organize sessions with clear goals, structured activities, and measurable outcomes. It ensures each session builds toward long-term player and team development.

Key Components of a Training Plan

Session Objective

Define the main focus of the session, such as passing, defending, finishing, or team shape.

Warm-Up

Prepare players with dynamic movement, stretching, and light ball work to reduce injury risk and improve readiness.

Main Drills

Include technical and tactical exercises that directly support the session objective.

Game-Based Activity

Use small-sided games to apply skills in realistic match situations.

Cool Down

End with stretching and recovery exercises, along with a short session review.

Example Training Session

Phase Time Activity Focus
Warm-Up 10 min Jogging, stretching, ball touches Prepare body and coordination
Technical Drill 20 min Passing and receiving drills Ball control and accuracy
Tactical Drill 20 min Positioning and movement exercise Game awareness
Small-Sided Game 25 min 4v4 or 5v5 match Apply skills under pressure
Cool Down 5 min Stretching and feedback Recovery and reflection

Benefits of Using a Training Planner

  • Improves session organization and efficiency
  • Ensures consistent player development
  • Helps track progress over time
  • Balances workload and recovery
  • Connects training sessions to match performance

Coaching Tips

  • Keep sessions focused on one main objective.
  • Use drills that simulate real match situations.
  • Adjust intensity based on player age and fitness level.
  • Encourage communication and teamwork.
  • Review each session and plan improvements for the next one.
