Player Tracking

Monitor player development throughout the season by tracking attendance, performance, fitness, skills, and personal goals.

What You Can Track

Attendance

Record which players attend training sessions and matches. Consistent attendance helps coaches understand commitment, availability, and team reliability.

Fitness & Workload

Track endurance, speed, agility, recovery, and training intensity to help prevent injuries and improve conditioning.

Technical Skills

Monitor passing, dribbling, shooting, first touch, ball control, and defensive technique over time.

Match Performance

Review goals, assists, tackles, saves, communication, positioning, and decision-making during games.

Player Development Areas

  • Passing accuracy and decision-making
  • Dribbling confidence and ball control
  • Shooting technique and finishing ability
  • Defensive positioning and tackling
  • Speed, stamina, agility, and strength
  • Leadership, communication, and teamwork

How Coaches Can Use Tracking

Player tracking helps coaches make better training decisions. By reviewing player data, coaches can identify strengths, spot areas for improvement, create personalized development plans, and adjust training sessions based on team needs.

Example Tracking Notes

Player: Alex Martinez

Strength: Strong passing and quick decision-making.

Needs Improvement: Defensive positioning and stamina.

Coach Action: Add more defensive shape drills and conditioning work.

Tracking Tips

  • Update player notes after each training session or match.
  • Use clear, measurable goals for each player.
  • Compare progress over weeks instead of judging one session.
  • Balance statistics with coach observations.
  • Give players feedback they can understand and act on.
